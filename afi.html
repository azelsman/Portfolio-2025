Challenge

  • American Family Insurance’s digital sales experience was fragmented across multiple legacy systems, leading to inconsistent interactions and customer confusion.
  • Different usage patterns drove unique drop-off behaviors: some customers were shoppers comparing quotes, others were ready to buy, some used the tool to validate agent-provided quotes, and others reached “hard stops” requiring agent support.
  • Lack of behavioral insight made it difficult to tailor the experience to user intent or identify when to transition from digital to human assistance.
  • Distributed UX and product teams worked in parallel with limited access to direct customer feedback, creating misalignment and redundant efforts.
  • Digital satisfaction scores lagged behind competitors, signaling the need for a cohesive, insight-driven strategy that unified the self-service experience end to end.

Understanding how customers used — and abandoned — the journey became the foundation for redesigning AmFam’s digital sales platform around clarity, intent, and measurable performance.

A talented remote team, scattered across tools and time zones — before Design Thinking brought focus and connection.

Vision & Objectives

  • Unite a distributed UX organization through virtual collaboration and a shared innovation mindset.
  • Leverage AI and modern frameworks to accelerate discovery, streamline workflows, and simplify complex offerings.
  • Build a scalable foundation for clarity, creativity, and continuous improvement across teams.

Anchored in collaboration, clarity, and design-led transformation — a vision that guided both team culture and enterprise impact.

Approach

  • Introduced Design Thinking frameworks adapted for remote environments — running virtual workshops in Miro, MURAL, and Figma so cross-functional teams could ideate and align in real time.
  • Leveraged AI-assisted discovery to synthesize insights from behavioral analytics, market research, and existing data, enabling evidence-based decisions without traditional user interviews.
  • Developed AI-driven “North Star” chat prototypes — conceptual conversational experiences that helped envision how customers might navigate complex insurance options. While not implemented, these visionary artifacts shaped long-term digital strategy.

By blending human-centered design with emerging AI tools, the team accelerated discovery, improved collaboration, and created a shared vision for the future of digital sales.

Transformation

  • Remote silos gave way to active, engaged collaboration across disciplines and locations.
  • AI tools automated routine work, freeing time for strategic design and experimentation.
  • Visionary prototypes aligned teams around a shared future — showing how AI could bring clarity to complex insurance offerings.

The shift from fragmented workflows to a unified, insight-driven culture redefined how AmFam’s teams collaborated, designed, and delivered customer value.
