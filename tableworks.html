Tableworks

Project Overview

  • Role & Timeline: Founder / Product Designer, Oct 2017 – Nov 2019
  • Scope: Browser‑based 3D configurator, design‑to‑manufacturing pipeline, MVP blueprint
  • Outcome: Validated concept, documented MVP, and scalable foundations for expansion

Tools & Methods

  • Strategic framing: Lean Startup Canvas, traction roadmap, problem/opportunity statements
  • Research: Interviews (friends/family, Craigslist, intercepts), field studies, market research
  • Analysis: SWOT, empathy mapping, journey mapping/workshops
  • Ideation: JTBD, HMW, Five Whys, storyboards, gamification explorations
  • Prototyping & testing: Lo‑fi → hi‑fi Axure with 3D integration; guerrilla/remote usability testing, contextual testing
  • Design: Figma, Adobe CC, After Effects, Axure, Miro
  • Visualization & 3D: Lucidchart, Sketchfab API, Unity, three.js, CAD, Maya
  • Collaboration: Jira, Confluence, Zoom, Teams

Background

Allan Zelsman founded Tableworks to solve a common furniture problem—limited meaningful customization. Combining a woodworking background with professional 3D/animation and an understanding of affordable manufacturing, he set out to let customers design furniture online and have it fabricated and delivered within weeks.

Early research showed shoppers loved tailoring pieces to their vision but lacked simple tools. Partnering with a software engineer, Allan built a proof‑of‑concept demonstrating real‑time customization to secure initial buy‑in.

Objectives

  • Empower customers: Real‑time customization of dimensions, materials, and finishes.
  • Bridge digital ↔ physical: Seamless pipeline from browser configuration to manufacturable outputs.
  • Validate MVP: Prove desirability/feasibility and define a repeatable blueprint for scale.

Challenges

  • 3D → production integration: Export manufacturable models without manual rework.
  • Resource constraints: Single engineer and pre‑seed budget required ruthless MVP scoping.
  • User adoption: Teach non‑technical shoppers 3D controls and build trust in custom, unseen products.
  • Market mindset: Shift from passive browsing to active design while addressing cost, returns, and quality.

Approach & Process

  • Frame & align: Lean Canvas, traction roadmap, clear problem/opportunity statements and success criteria.
  • Discover & synthesize: 30+ interviews/field studies; insights mapped with empathy maps and SWOT.
  • Ideate & prototype: Storyboards and lo‑fi wires → hi‑fi Axure prototype with embedded 3D (Sketchfab).
  • Test & iterate: Remote and contextual usability tests refined controls and workflows.
  • Validate & plan: Exported designs to CAD for manufacturability; scoped CNC/3D‑printing integrations for MVP.

Solutions

  • Custom 3D configurator: Browser interface to adjust dimensions, choose materials/finishes, and see instant 3D updates (Axure + Sketchfab).
  • Design‑to‑manufacturing pipeline: Automated export to CAD/CAM with cut‑lists and fabrication instructions for CNC and 3D‑printing—no manual translation.
  • Interactive prototype suite: Lo‑fi Figma + hi‑fi Axure with full 3D integration to validate customization flows pre‑build.
  • Onboarding & trust builders: Starter templates, contextual tooltips, transparent pricing/timelines to reduce first‑time friction.

Together these formed a validated MVP framework connecting digital design, UX, and production into a seamless, user‑driven furniture platform.

Impact & Results

  • Concept validation: 30+ target users interviewed/tested; three personas identified; strong appetite for on‑demand customization.
  • Prototype success: Hi‑fi Axure + 3D cut onboarding time ~50% and clarified dimension/material controls.
  • MVP blueprint: Lean Canvas, roadmap, requirements, brand narrative, and Figma‑based design system.
  • Automated pipeline: End‑to‑end CAD/CAM export enabled direct handoff to CNC and 3D‑printing.
  • Scalable foundations: Reusable UI library and Lean/Design‑Thinking playbook to accelerate future work.

Lessons Learned

  • Iterate early with manufacturability checks to surface constraints before expensive builds.
  • Cross‑discipline collaboration (UX, 3D, woodworking, additive manufacturing) creates feasible solutions.
  • Education and transparent pricing/tooltips are key to trust and adoption.
  • Stay lean; pair MVP focus with a reusable design system to scale efficiently.

Next Steps & Recommendations

  • Scale manufacturing partners: Formalize CNC/3D‑printing vendors; expand materials.
  • Feature roadmap: AR previews, mobile configurator, light gamification (quizzes, rewards).
  • Seed fundraising: Use POC traction/pilot interest to fund a production‑ready platform.
  • Metrics & analytics: Instrument onboarding, design‑to‑order conversion, time‑to‑first‑design.
  • Team & process: Hire engineering + PM + manufacturing liaison; formalize agile CD pipelines.
  • Go‑to‑market: Target early adopters via design communities, influencer partners, and beta invites.
