myUSPTO

Project Overview

  • Client: U.S. Patent & Trademark Office (USPTO)
  • Role & Timeline: Sr. UX Designer / Information Architect (Lead), Aug 2014 – Sept 2015
  • Scope: SSO portal, personalized dashboard, widget framework
  • Standards: FedRAMP, WCAG 2.0 / Section 508

Tools & Methods

  • Axure
  • Adobe Photoshop / Illustrator
  • User & stakeholder interviews
  • Workshops / whiteboarding
  • Requirements analysis & data analytics
  • IA, interaction design, prototyping

Background

USPTO replaced paper/fax login with Patent Authentication Control (PAC). myUSPTO provides a personalized dashboard and single sign‑on to USPTO systems, with an expanding widget library for docketing, document management, and collaboration.

Objectives

  • Unify access: One branded SSO portal for internal users, agencies, and stakeholders.
  • Enable teams: Workflow, case management, and granular permissions in a secure environment.
  • Increase efficiency: Personalized alerts/notifications via dashboard widgets.
  • Ensure compliance: Meet FedRAMP and WCAG 2.0 / Section 508.
  • Scale: Support future widgets and platform expansion.

Challenges

  • Disparate authentication: Multiple legacy logins caused security and audit gaps.
  • Security & compliance: MFA + 508/WCAG needs without degrading UX.
  • Legacy integration: Non‑integrated PAC and migrating CMS to unify.
  • Stakeholder alignment: Patent vs. Trademark workflows and policies.
  • Responsive modularity: Device‑agnostic UI and future tool integration under constraints.

Approach & Process

  • Discovery & research: Interviews, workshops, whiteboarding to map pain points, access, and dashboards.
  • Definition: Journey maps, “must‑haves,” access diagrams; identified widgets (docketing, alerts, tasks, collaboration).
  • Prototyping & testing: Axure flows for SSO, personalization, and widgets; device‑wide stakeholder testing and iteration.
  • Systemization: Centralized design system for consistency, accessibility, and scalability.

Solutions

  • myUSPTO SSO portal: Secure login with a personalized dashboard.
  • Widget ecosystem: Docket, Alerts, Task Manager, Case Status—modular and extensible.
  • Accessibility & security: FedRAMP and WCAG 2.0/508‑compliant patterns and handoffs.

Impact & Results

  • Security & simplicity: ~62% fewer password‑reset/auth incidents post‑release; audit issues eliminated.
  • Efficiency: ~45% faster access to case alerts per user/year after launch.
  • Adoption: 7,500+ agency users in year one; “Docket” widget piloted process improvements.
  • Scalability: Platform expanded with additional widgets and continued use across USPTO apps.

Lessons Learned

  • Engage stakeholders early and often to build buy‑in.
  • Balance security with usability through UCD.
  • Systemize for consistency; make compliance continuous.
  • Design with broad stakeholder involvement to future‑proof.

Next Steps & Recommendations

  • Expand widgets: Grow library and adoption metrics.
  • Governance & audits: Ongoing security reviews and feedback loops.
  • Accessibility: Maintain WCAG/508 with automated + real‑user testing.
  • Platform strategy: Reuse modular widget framework across future USPTO portals.
