What We Shipped

  • Unified authentication across 3 business units — one USPTO ID with MFA, single recovery, single session.
  • Account linking & permissions — attorneys/agents and customers manage multiple portfolios from one place.
  • Task-oriented dashboard — deadlines, office actions, filings, and fees for cases/trademarks/patents in one view.
  • Cross-system alerts & reminders — status changes and due dates surfaced in-product (and via email where supported).
  • Consolidated filings & receipts — drafts, submissions, and payments centralized for easier follow-through.

Starting Point

Each business unit had its own sign-in (including legacy fob-based MFA). Only one user could own a case—so people shared logins, increasing risk and support tickets.

  • Problem: Fragmented authentication and single-owner cases → bottlenecks and credential sharing.
  • Constraints: Legacy MFA (fobs), siloed systems, strict compliance/privacy.
  • Who we served: Examiners, attorneys/agents, paralegals, and pro se filers.

Vision & Objectives

Give customers one secure way to sign in and one place to work.

  • Unify authentication across 3 BUs → single USPTO ID with MFA and delegated access.
  • Provide a task-oriented home for cases, trademarks, and patents → deadlines, filings, and fees in one view.
  • Reduce risk and support load → eliminate shared credentials; meet compliance/privacy requirements.

Leadership Moves

  • Stakeholder alignment — interviews, workshops, SME whiteboards → shared priorities.
  • North star — “one portal, many roles” guided dashboard + widget strategy.
  • Continuous compliance — FedRAMP + WCAG/508 baked into patterns and reviews.
  • Decision cadence — shared criteria + weekly governance to converge on one direction.

Innovation in Action

  • Discovery & research — mapped auth flows and role needs; identified high-value dashboard widgets.
  • Definition & prioritization — focused roadmap on Docket, Alerts, Tasks, and Case Status with role-aware behaviors.
  • Prototyping & testing — Axure prototypes for SSO, personalization, and widgets; iterated via stakeholder walkthroughs and usability checks.
  • Systemization — accessible design system and documentation to ensure consistency and accelerate extensions.

Key Solutions

  • myUSPTO SSO Portal — unified, secure login with a personalized landing that centralizes tools and info.
  • Modular widget ecosystem — Docket, Alerts, Task Manager, Case Status as plug-and-play, role-aware modules for rapid expansion.
  • Accessibility & security by design — FedRAMP + WCAG/508 baked into patterns, reviews, and the design system.
  • Scalable IA & governance — information architecture and contribution guidelines to onboard new widgets, teams, and policies.

Lessons Learned

  • Engage stakeholders early and often to build durable buy‑in.
  • Balance security with usability through iterative, user‑centered testing.
  • Systemize accessibility and compliance to prevent downstream rework.
  • Design modularly to future‑proof against policy, product, and platform shifts.

Forward Look

  • Expand the widget library and deepen metrics on adoption and efficiency.
  • Maintain ongoing security and accessibility audits with clear governance.
  • Continue real‑user testing alongside analytics to validate outcomes.
  • Reuse the modular framework for adjacent USPTO portals and partner integrations.
