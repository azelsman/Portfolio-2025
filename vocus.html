Vocus Marketing Cloud

Project Overview

  • Client: Vocus
  • Role & Timeline: Director of User Experience, Feb 2011 – Jan 2013
  • Team & Stakeholders: Solo UX lead → grew to 4+ designers; 5 Product Owners; Engineering leads; Executive stakeholders

Tools & Methods

  • User‑centered design
  • Adobe CC
  • Collaboration workshops
  • Personas, journeys, IA, workflows
  • Lean UX (2‑week discovery–design–validate sprints)

Background

Vocus was a leading SaaS provider of PR, Social Media, Analytics, and Email Marketing tools. After multiple acquisitions, the Marketing Cloud became a set of siloed modules with inconsistent interfaces and fragmented workflows.

  • Business challenge: Teams lacked a unified KPI dashboard (sentiment, engagement, campaign performance), driving training costs, support tickets, and manual reconciliation.
  • UX mandate: Stand up a UX practice, embed research, define a cohesive design language and component library, and incrementally integrate modules into one coherent suite.
  • Team & collaboration: Started solo; scaled to four designers; partnered with Product, Engineering, and acquired design leads to deliver two MVP releases under one UX framework.

Objectives

  • Unify & streamline: Integrate PR, Social, and Email with a single KPI dashboard to drive cross‑sell and reduce support/training.
  • Seamless workflows: Consistent authoring, scheduling, and publishing to minimize context switching.
  • Rapid delivery: Ship two MVP‑level releases in 12 months via 2‑week Lean UX sprints.
  • Scalable UX foundation: Centralized design system + continuous research for data‑driven decisions.

Challenges

  • Fragmented ecosystem: Siloed legacy/new modules with inconsistent patterns created debt and confusion.
  • Cross‑functional alignment: Unite acquired teams around a shared design language and roadmap while balancing priorities.
  • Scope under MVP constraints: Tight prioritization to hit two releases in 12 months.
  • Scaling the UX practice: Onboard designers; institute design‑ops (components, specs, reviews) for consistency.
  • Speed vs. quality: Maintain usability and research rigor while accelerating delivery.

Approach & Process

  • Lean‑agile sprints: 2‑week discovery–design–validate cycles to iterate and manage scope.
  • Discovery & prioritization: 15+ interviews, 8 workshops → personas, journeys, and ~30‑story prioritized backlog.
  • Iterative ideation: Low‑fi sketches, IA, workflows progressing to high‑fi blueprints.
  • Design‑ops handoff: Process to keep developers on the latest designs across the unified UX.
  • Phase II onboarding & scaling: Integrated an acquired team into shared processes; scaled UX 1 → 4 designers.

Solutions

  • Single KPI dashboard: “Pane of glass” with customizable sentiment, engagement, and campaign widgets.
  • Unified navigation: Global header with Plan / Publish / Analyze mega‑menus, breadcrumbs, and in‑app search.
  • Streamlined authoring: One Create modal for press releases, social posts, and emails; guided, component‑driven forms.
  • Real‑time insights: Header alerts for mentions/spikes with one‑click drilldowns to analytics.
  • Design‑ops handoff: Storybook‑style docs + weekly paired reviews in Jira/Figma for incremental delivery.

Impact & Results

  • Rapid delivery: Two end‑to‑end MVP releases in 12 months; hit aggressive roadmap targets.
  • Operational efficiency: Support load dropped; training moved from multi‑day to self‑serve in‑app guidance.
  • User & stakeholder satisfaction: “Smoother workflows,” “fewer context switches,” “clearer hand‑offs.”
  • Organizational growth: UX established as strategic; team scaled 1 → 4; Lean UX embedded across lines.

Lessons Learned

  • Align stakeholders early to avoid late pivots.
  • Lightweight governance enforces consistency but adapts to context.
  • Continuous research and analytics every sprint earn buy‑in.
  • Two‑week Lean cycles keep scope tight and quality high.
  • Culture matters: pair reviews, critiques, and rituals are essential when scaling.
  • Modular design system speeds delivery and new‑hire ramp‑up.

Next Steps & Recommendations

  • Define & track success: KPIs for adoption, support tickets, task completion; instrument dashboards.
  • Institutionalize cadence: Maintain 2‑week discovery–design–validate sprints with ongoing tests & analytics.
  • Quarterly stakeholder workshops: Review performance, validate priorities, capture emerging needs.
  • Scale & sustain UX: Invest in onboarding, design‑ops automation, and training to support growth and quality.
