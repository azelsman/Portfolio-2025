CGI Momentum

Project Overview

  • Client: CGI Momentum
  • Role & Timeline: Senior Product Designer, Sep 2015 – Nov 2017
  • Team & Stakeholders: 8 devs, 2 PMs, 2 designers, executive stakeholders

Tools & Methods

  • Adobe
  • Axure
  • User interviews
  • Usability testing
  • Design Thinking workshops

Background

CGI Momentum (pre 2015–Nov 2017) was a legacy, monolithic web app supporting federal/state accounting workflows. A decade of feature creep resulted in:

  • Redundant taxonomies and navigation screens
  • 5‑tier nav with 3–7 related objects taking up to 4 clicks
  • Non‑standard form layouts/controls impacting success
  • Performance bottlenecks across multiple screens

No dedicated UX led to low adoption, high support costs, and stalled innovation.

Objectives

  • Modernize on MVC with reusable modules
  • Cut support tickets ≥ 50% via onboarding, search, and help
  • Improve usability with consistent patterns/standards
  • Increase flexibility using tabs, filters, faceted results
  • Build trust via clear validation/errors and queuing
  • Enable self‑service with doc repo, status, and a pattern library

Challenges

  • Cross‑functional constraints: Competing priorities, entrenched silos, and skepticism toward UX.
  • Stakeholder buy‑in: “Speed‑listening” tours + support‑ticket analysis → executive cases for UX investment.
  • Embedded UX mindset: Lean UX metrics, biweekly critiques, and “Speed Testing” to hard‑wire feedback into releases.

Approach & Process

  • Discovery & alignment: Workshops and SME interviews to create empathy maps and personas.
  • Prioritization & roadmap: Top 12 pain points (~80% friction) → phased backlog with design/product/dev.
    Prototypes (Adobe/Axure) tested in “Speed Testing”; insights fed to sprint retros.
  • Systemization & rollout: Migrated to a 508‑compliant React component library (MVC).
    Daily standups + biweekly design critiques for continuous UX feedback.

Solutions

  • Guided onboarding & contextual help: Help overlays, tooltips, and tutorials cut new‑user errors by >50%.
  • Advanced search & faceting: Cross‑library search by taxonomy/type/status with inline error handling; thousands of records accessible in seconds.
  • User dashboard: Unified tasks, alerts, deadlines, and quick links to minimize deep navigation.
  • Enterprise design system & pattern library: 508‑compliant React components (HTML/CSS/JS, icons, theming) integrated into legacy & new builds (Bitbucket). Hi‑fi React prototype + user stories + Confluence blueprints for consistent handoff.

Impact & Results

  • Modernization: Multi‑brand React library → foundation for enterprise design system.
  • Delivery: On time and budget; broad adoption.
  • Support: ~50% fewer tickets via onboarding/help/error handling.
  • Insights: Lean UX + “Speed Testing” sustained a multi‑release feedback loop.

Lessons Learned

  • Scale with systems; DS accelerates ideation and validation.
  • Use mid/hi‑fi prototypes early to surface gaps and build trust.
  • Anchor priorities in metrics, journeys, and SME insights.
  • Maintain frequent exec touchpoints to keep momentum.
  • Introduce change incrementally; co‑create to reduce risk.

Next Steps & Recommendations

  • Governance: Design Standards Council for cadence, requests, and quality.
  • Extend DS: Modular layouts, mobile variants, dashboards/search/export.
  • Analytics + feedback: Pair product analytics with ongoing “Speed Testing.”
  • Accessibility: Quarterly 508 reviews + automated and real‑user testing.
