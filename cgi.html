Starting Point

Before 2015, Momentum was a monolithic web app supporting complex federal/state accounting workflows. Years of feature creep created complexity, inconsistency, and performance drag—with no dedicated UX to guide the product.

  • Redundant taxonomies and 5‑tier navigation; 3–7 related objects often took up to 4 clicks.
  • Non‑standard form layouts/controls reduced task success.
  • Performance bottlenecks across key screens.
  • Low adoption and high support costs; stalled innovation.

Prioritizing stuff to fix was like Whac-a-Mole — no clear plan, onboarding was difficult, and constant issues that left teams resistant to upgrades.

A cluttered, outdated interface made simple tasks slow and confusing — every click felt like guesswork.

After discovery, workflows were redesigned around real tasks — helping examiners stay organized and on top of backlogs.

Vision & Objectives

  • Modernize on reusable components to speed delivery and consistency
  • Cut support load and time-to-complete by guiding core tasks.
  • Standardize patterns, search, and help to reduce training and rework.

Product Ownership Moves

  • Set the UX north star and acceptance criteria for each release.
  • Ran discovery → prioritization → sprint goals with PM & Eng.
  • Owned roadmap trade-offs (expert density vs guidance).
  • Approved readiness: prototypes → usability → dev sign-off.

What we Shipped

  • Discovered 12 pain points → 80% of friction and aligned on what to fix first.
  • Prioritized the backlog with PM/Eng and sequenced four waves.
  • Prototyped in short loops with users; insights fed each sprint.
  • Standardized UI with a 508-compliant React library for speed and consistency.
  • Delivered guided workflows, search, and contextual help across acquisition and planning.

Lessons Learned

  • Scale innovation by building systems, not just screens.
  • Use mid/high‑fidelity prototypes early to surface gaps and build trust.
  • Anchor priorities in metrics, journeys, and SME insights.
  • Maintain frequent executive touchpoints to keep momentum.
  • Introduce change incrementally; co‑create to reduce risk.

Forward Look

  • Design Standards Council: Establish a Design Standards Council for governance, cadence, and quality.
  • Design System Expansion: Expand the design system with modular layouts, mobile variants, and richer dashboards/search/export.
  • Product Analytics & Speed Testing: Pair product analytics with ongoing “Speed Testing” to validate impact.
  • Quarterly 508 Reviews: Run quarterly 508 reviews with automated and real‑user testing.
